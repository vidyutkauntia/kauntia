The short version

From a single service station to a 449-strong electric fleet — built one disciplined depot at a time.

Kauntia Motor Works operates and maintains electric buses for Switch Mobility and JBM across Karnataka, Maharashtra, Delhi and Assam — while maintaining ~450 government ICE buses in West Bengal, a Tata Motors CV service station, and two Royal Enfield outlets. We grew from zero to 300+ e-buses in roughly 12 months.

2006 → today

Two decades on the road

Follow the road down. Where it turns green, we turned electric.

  1. 2006

    The foundation

    A Tata-authorised commercial-vehicle service station opens in West Bengal. The operating discipline starts here.

  2. 2008

    First fleet contracts

    On-site servicing begins for the West Bengal Transport Corporation — the start of large public-fleet maintenance.

  3. 2013–19

    Scaling ICE maintenance

    Grew to 400+ government buses across 13 depots — engine overhauls, preventive maintenance, hard-won depot know-how.

  4. 2020

    Built for scale

    Workshop capacity doubled to 12 bays, fully BS-VI compliant — the platform ready for what came next.

  5. ⚡ The electric pivot2023

    Going electric

    New venture: 120 Switch e-buses in Bengaluru. Zero to a running electric depot — the road turns green.

  6. 2024

    Multi-state operator

    61 Switch e-buses at Dolvi, Maharashtra — and India's first female charging operators.

  7. 2025

    National footprint

    168 JBM e-buses in Delhi — disciplined operations now span the country, OEM to OEM.

  8. 2026

    Into the East

    Backed operations in Guwahati for 100 EV buses — extending the platform into a new region as a 50,000-bus national pipeline consolidates toward disciplined operators.
