The short version

One service station in 2006. A 449-bus electric fleet today. Built one disciplined depot at a time.

We run and maintain electric buses for Switch Mobility and JBM across four states, keep roughly 450 government buses moving in West Bengal, and operate a Tata Motors service station with two Royal Enfield outlets. Zero to 300 plus e-buses in about 12 months.

2006 to today

Two decades on the road

Follow the road down. Where it turns green, we turned electric.

  1. 2006

    The foundation

    A Tata authorised commercial vehicle service station opens in West Bengal. The operating discipline starts here.

  2. 2008

    First fleet contracts

    On-site servicing begins for the West Bengal Transport Corporation. Public fleets, at scale.

  3. 2013–19

    Scaling ICE maintenance

    Up to 400 plus government buses across 13 depots. Engine overhauls, preventive maintenance, deep depot know-how.

  4. 2020

    Built for scale

    Workshop capacity doubles to 12 bays, fully BS-VI compliant. The platform is ready.

  5. The electric pivot2023

    Going electric

    New venture, fast start. 120 Switch e-buses in Bengaluru. The road turns green.

  6. 2024

    Multi-state operator

    61 Switch e-buses at Dolvi, Maharashtra. And India's first female charging operators.

  7. 2025

    National footprint

    168 JBM e-buses in Delhi. Disciplined operations, OEM to OEM, across the country.

  8. 2026

    Into the East

    Operations backed in Guwahati for 100 EV buses. A 50,000-bus national pipeline is consolidating toward operators who deliver. We already run like one.
